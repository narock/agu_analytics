Scientometrics is the science of science. Scientometric research areas include measurements of impact, mapping of scientific fields, and the production of indicators for use in policy and management. This site leverages scientometric principles to study the American Geophysical Union (AGU). Using the AGU's Linked Data Abstract Browser we have created a number of visualizations and analytics tools to explore the historical AGU network.

The data and code used in this project is publicly available at our Open Science Framework site. Questions/comments/suggestions? Reach out to us at tnarock AT ndm.edu or @tnarock on Twitter.

Part of our analysis is based on uniquely identifying people. However, people can change affiliations and email addresses. In addition, varying user interfaces lead to varying information being collected. For example, some systems require first initial and last name while others use full names. Even within one system, such as AGU, people identify themselves differently over time. Automatically identifying multiple instances of the same person in our software is a challenge. To help with this, we've set up a simple form where you can tell us if you've used multiple email addresses over the years and also if you have an ORCID id. Supplying these simple bits of information will go a long way in automating person disambiguation
